Intro

My story begins in Taiyuan, Shanxi Province, China (Central China), a place that has shaped my perspective and fueled my passion for understanding societal dynamics. This journey has led me to Peking University, where I'm currently a Ph.D. candidate at the Institute of Linguistics and Applied Linguistics, School of Foreign Languages, with an expected graduation in June or December 2028. Previously, I completed my Bachelor's in Translation (Sep.2018——Jun.2022) and Master's in Linguistics (Sep.2022——Jun.2024) at the School of International Studies, University of International Business and Economics, Beijing, China.

At the heart of my research, I employ quantitative methods to delve into language use and uncover the social cognitive motivations that drive it. I'm deeply invested in applying interdisciplinary insights and mixed methods (triangulation) to enrich discourse analysis and pragmatics studies. My previous work has touched upon topics like corporate discourse, social media, professional communication, academic writing, political narratives, and health literacy. My evolving interests now also include environmental communication in business and media contexts, public-oriented science communication, and AI4SS (AI for Social Science). I approach these subjects, particularly those concerning China, with an international lens and a focus on comparative analysis.
